# Visual Style Guide

## Default Visual Direction

- Keep silhouettes readable on mobile.
- Keep face and hands visible during important actions.
- Avoid overly busy backgrounds.
- Use consistent character proportions and color palettes.
- Use expressive poses, not static talking heads.

## Camera Language

- Hook shots: medium close-up or clear action shot.
- Dialogue: medium shot, over-shoulder, or two-shot.
- Punchline: hold long enough for the reaction to register.
- Escalation: push-in, quick cut, or reaction insert.

## Transitions

Approved MVP transitions:

- hard cut;
- match cut;
- sound bridge;
- push-in cut;
- whip pan;
- quick reaction cutaway.

Avoid:

- random dissolves;
- transitions that hide continuity errors;
- sudden location changes without visual or audio bridge.

## Captions

- Captions must not cover faces, hands, key props, or punchline visuals.
- Use short lines.
- Prefer 1-2 lines at a time.
- Keep caption timing aligned to voice.
