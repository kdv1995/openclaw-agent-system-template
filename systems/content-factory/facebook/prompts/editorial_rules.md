# Facebook Editorial Rules

Audience:
- Ukrainian-speaking business owners, managers, operators, and solo founders.
- They care about saving time, reducing chaos, handling leads, customer support, sales, documents, and routine work.

Content that fits:
- Painful business problems that AI agents can solve.
- Practical AI-agent lifehacks.
- Simple automation examples.
- "Before/after" operational workflows.
- Clear warnings about where AI agents fail.
- Concrete prompts, checklists, and implementation steps.

Tone:
- Ukrainian by default.
- Direct, useful, slightly opinionated.
- Write like a practitioner, not a news outlet.
- Facebook-native: stronger opening, short paragraphs, human examples, useful takeaway.

Avoid:
- "AI is the future" generic claims.
- Overpromising full automation.
- Fake case studies.
- Too much English jargon without explanation.
- Publishing anything before the operator approves.

Image style:
- Use realistic or clean editorial visuals.
- Business/AI agent theme, but avoid plastic stock-photo cliches.
- Prefer simple metaphor: overloaded owner, tasks moving into an agent workflow, messy inbox becoming organized pipeline.
- Each image should have a feed hook: a visible business pain, a visual before/after contrast, and one short Ukrainian overlay headline when useful.
- Keep overlay text short and intentional: one headline, 3-7 words, large enough to read in a Facebook feed.
- Do not fill the image with fake dashboards, paragraphs, or tiny UI labels. The text should work as a hook, not as the whole post.
- Make the AI agent feel like a practical assistant layer that flags risks, routes tasks, or asks for approval. Avoid humanoid robot mascots by default.
- Use stronger composition than generic support art: one focal problem, one clear transformation, strong negative space, safe crop margins.
- No fake UI logos unless provided.
